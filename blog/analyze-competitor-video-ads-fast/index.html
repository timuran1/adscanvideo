How to Analyze Competitor Video Ads in 60 Seconds

Ad Intelligence · July 2026 · 4 min read

Most marketers watch a competitor's video ad and think, "That looks good" — then move on. But the best performing ads follow specific patterns. Hook timing. Pacing. Visual language. CTA positioning. If you can read those signals, you can replicate what works and avoid what doesn't.

Here's a framework for analyzing any video ad systematically — and how to do it in about 60 seconds with the right tool.

The 5-Point Ad Analysis Framework

1. The Hook (First 3 Seconds)

The first 3 seconds determine whether someone scrolls past or watches. Look for:

  • Visual hook: A striking image, unexpected movement, or bold text overlay
  • Verbal hook: A question, bold claim, or relatable pain point
  • Pacing: Fast cuts to grab attention, or slow for premium feel?

Most high-performing ads use both a visual and verbal hook within the first second.

2. Structure & Pacing

Map the ad's timeline. Count the scene changes. How long does each scene last?

  • Problem → Solution → CTA: Classic structure, works for most products
  • Story arc: Character-driven, emotional, slower pacing
  • Fast cuts (1-2 sec per scene): Energy, urgency, younger audience
  • Slow dissolves (4-6 sec per scene): Premium, luxury, considered purchases

3. Visual Language

Every visual choice communicates something:

  • Color palette — warm (trust, comfort) vs cool (tech, premium)
  • Camera movement — handheld (authentic) vs gimbal (polished)
  • Aspect ratio — vertical (TikTok/Reels) vs square (feed) vs landscape (YouTube)
  • Lighting — high-key (approachable) vs low-key (dramatic, premium)

4. Text Overlays & Captions

More than half of video ads are watched without sound. Top-performing ads always have:

  • Subtitles that pop visually (not just white text)
  • Key claims reinforced on screen
  • Brand name visible within 5 seconds

5. The CTA

How does the ad ask for action?

  • Direct: "Swipe up," "Click the link"
  • Soft: "Learn more," "See how it works"
  • Urgency: "Limited time," "Only X left"

💡 Pro tip: The best CTAs are visible for at least 2 seconds AND repeated in the voiceover. If you have to pause to read it, it's too fast.
